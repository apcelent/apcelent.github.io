Why do some girls(single child or the youngest sibling) act like guys?


It can be because of two reasons:
1. Their wishes have constantly been fulfilled, and they have been pampered a lot.
2. They have got into some sort of inferiority complex/unfortunate incidents and have survived it. Single Child might be lonely and wanting to express herself, and the younger sibling might have felt dominated by the elder one.

Case 1:
When everything was perfect, the youngest sibling/single child gets all the love from the family, almost everyone is at her service, for even small things.
The basic human urge for more, can never stop, masculinity or tom-boyish nature is just an extrapolation of the dominance which they subconsciously develop for the reason that all their beck and calls have been addressed.

Case 2:
If the younger sibling is not fortunate enough, and is bullied by the elder one(this might apply to the other children as well who are bullied), to every action there is equal and opposite reaction. If the child has been subjected to some incidents that would have left them feeling bullied and as a recovery mechanism they would have chosen to fight, rather than flight. Male being the dominant gender was the only choice that had been left, as a defense mechanism, resulting into a tom boyish nature.

In more severe cases the girl might refrain from doing any activity that reminds her of her female identity to the extent that she might start developing excuses for not getting married. Anything that would remind the girl of her female identity will be a turn off, for example pink color, dolls, weakness in all forms and even commitment.
