National Innovation Foundation has this competition called ignite, which mentors/promotes innovation of school children. more here Dr. Kalam was here to award the selected participants from across the country and the excerpts from his speech -

The challenge, my young friends, is that you have to fight the hardest
battle, which any human being can ever imagine to fight; and never stop fighting until you arrive at your destined place, that is, a UNIQUE YOU! Conclusion Dear young friends, I am happy that the winners of the ignite competition have already shown how to be unique with original ideas and creative and inclusive thoughts.



Many of these ideas have become products ready to go to market and I am very happy that I see in front me thousand of potential innovators who will make India impatient with problems and spur it on the path of inclusive development.



Creativity is a process through which, we can continuously improve ideas and find unique solutions by making gradual alterations and refinements to our works.



Friends, recently I was addressing the Indian Science congress at Jammu and reminded them that they have to start thinking about creative ideas at an early stage in life.



Creative person has an attitude to accept change and newness, a willingness to play with ideas and possibilities, a flexibility of outlook, the habit of enjoying the good, while looking for ways to improve it.



Dimensions of Knowledge Society In the knowledge economy serves the objective of a society change from fulfilling the basic needs of all-round development to empowerment.



Creativity In a knowledge society, we have to make innovations continuously.



Creative mind has the ability to imagine or invent something new by combining, changing or reapplying existing ideas.
