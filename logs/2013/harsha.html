For the first time, saw Harsha Bhogle in person. What draws me most to him, is his insightful articulation of Sachin, and of course those cricinfo articles as well. He came with a presentation, this article is a gist of all those slides, and the quotes, jokes and examples he delivered during the talk at a short visit to the IIM-A campus. He was also accompanied by the commentator Alan Willkins, who also happens to be a great guy, he as well narrated couple of stories.

Contains few generic sentences as well, ignore per your wish.

Be a part of a winning team.

Showed it via the example of the two contemporaries Md. Ashraful and Michael Clarke

How can I win the game vs. how can I save the game attitude.

1% most of the times differentiates success and failure.

Ricky Ponting was world's best batsman but he made sure he hits the stumps it was his 1%.

Chasing the ball till the boundary lines, running the singles hard, staying fit is 1%.

For Inzamam, running between the wickets was 1% which he did not do :-)

You need not worry too much about doing different things, rather than doing simple things right. If you are being hit start bowling line and length for the next 30 minutes. Remember Glenn Mc Grath.

Having read this, make a list of your 1% :-)

Giving 100% every time is the only way to live life.

We as Indians are obsessed with Jugaad. Its because we want the things get done, whichever way, because that gives us the results and avoids failure. Success is getting a century, but you don't score them, they happen. Jugaad is the enemy of excellence, and vice versa. Take the anxiety of the result away. You will enjoy the work and be more productive.

Adapt to all playing conditions.

Remember why Dravid and Tendulkar are great, it is because they scored runs overseas as well. There averages are similar irrespective of playing conditions. This is why Sangakara is a better player than Jayavardane.

On a lighter note, he mentioned true stories of Indian players getting injured after taking a look at the bouncy pitches, because fast bowlers are either angry or angrier.

"Only when the tide goes out do you discover who's been swimming naked." - Warren Buffet

Seek adversity to see how good you are, for adversity is an opportunity.

Success is always in the context of time, space and scale.

When you lose you analyze, when you win, you go for a drink. You need to celebrate failure and analyze success as well, to some extent. If you don't know, why you have succeeded you won't be able to replicate it.

What made you succeed in one environment, won't be the cause in the second context.

Resources vs Resourcefulness

You should learn to make best of what you have, because you can't have all that is required. Continuous improvement can take you there, the initial size is not everything.

The story of Rohit Sharma. If talent is all you have, you are poor. Ability is no guarantee to success, its your work ethic, that takes you there.

Don't let what you can't do prohibit from doing what you can do.

Eg. Jonty Rhodes, used to say his average is much less than that of Cullinan or Kirsten. But net-net he would save 20 runs in the field and had an average of around 35, which made the net average of 55, which in any given day is better than a batting average of 45 and giving out runs by mis-fielding.

Get out of your comfort zone

"Out hone se itna dar lagta hai, run kaise banayenge." - Azharuddin in a club match on couple of players. (They are so afraid of getting out, how will they score runs.)

What made you succeed is what you like doing, and you keep doing it again and again. You become afraid of trying new things, because there is a stigma attached with the failure.

The good thing about making mistakes is that, you make fewer mistakes after making mistakes. So be glad about committing mistakes.

"I have made more mistakes than anyone in the broadcasting industry." - Harsha

Team

Excellent people with individual egos will screw things. You need to dissolve personal ego, to build a better team. Clive Lloyd did a herculean job, he was able to manage egos, and that made the west indian team the best.

On a sports jersey, the badge in-front is much important than the name behind.
